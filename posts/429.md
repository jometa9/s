date: "01-11-2026"
title: "I stopped making videos"
---

Since 2022 I have a YouTube channel.

I started it at the same time I started my real coding career — studying, writing more code every day, with one goal: to get a job in IT as a software engineer.

The channel was me pushing my limits, going out of my comfort zone completely. And it checked off one of the pending to-dos I always carried in my life: making my own YouTube channel.

So I used it for that. A document of my learning, and a way to show my skills as a developer.

Some months I posted a video every day.

Other months the channel just disappeared, and then I came back and started creating again.

At some point the channel became a responsibility.

But here is the part I had to be honest about. My income was never YouTube. 

The channel is just a document. 

A document with a weekly routine attached to it.

So I asked myself one thing: do I still need a channel to document my life?

Right now, for who I am and what I want, the answer is no. 

My life changed. My objectives changed.

The channel was built for an older version of me, and that weekly routine doesn't fit anymore.

So I started looking for another way to keep the reports and the logs of my life. 

A simple way. Not for an audience. Just for me.

No camera. No editing. No upload on Sunday.

Just writing.

This is that. From here, the document is the writing.
