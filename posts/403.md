## What Is a Behavioral Interview?
 
When you're looking for new jobs in the IT world, most hiring processes have multiple steps. Depending on the position and the organization, each has its own process and rules. But I'm discovering a pattern.
 
One key step in this process is the "screening" or **behavioral interview** — the "let's get to know each other" meeting.
 
In this meeting, we don't speak about technical questions. Instead, we discuss your background as a professional. In my case, as a developer.
 
**Topics that typically come up:**
- What types of projects have you worked on?
- What's your current job situation?
- Have you led a team or had people under your responsibility?
- What's the worst situation you've faced in your job?
- What's the most challenging thing you've dealt with?
In this post, I write my own presentation and answers to specific questions that come up in these meetings.

## The Most Challenging Thing I've Done
 
Building my own software as a service as proof of my skills has been the most challenging thing—and the most rewarding.
 
The SaaS is called **5MTrader**. It's a copy-trading platform for CFD and Forex traders. This is different from social trading. Social trading is when one trader copies the trades of another profitable trader. 5MTrader is for a single trader's internal accounts—copying trades from one account to another within the same person's portfolio.
 
This type of copying is popular in the CFD and Forex world.
 
Beyond copy trading, 5MTrader also includes:
- **Stats and analytics** — detailed performance tracking
- **AI Coach** — based on the trading history of the user
- **Backtester** — a simulated environment where traders can validate their strategy and learn without risking money on real markets

## How 5MTrader Works (Technical Overview)
 
### Integrations
 
5MTrader integrates with:
- **MetaTrader 5 and cTrader** — for the copy-trading system
- **TradingView** — for the backtester functionality and charting
### Architecture
 
The **landing page and basic API** are in the same project using **Next.js** (Next.js has this feature where you can add API routes in the same project).
 
For the copy-trading system itself, we use external servers depending on the user's plan:
- **Some users:** Copy engine on AWS
- **Others:** Copy engine on Contabo
### AWS vs. Contabo Trade-off
 
**Main difference: Price**
 
Contabo is sometimes 10x cheaper than AWS. But there's a disadvantage: With Contabo, you need to manually manage the infrastructure. You have to monitor CPU and RAM consumption manually and build custom logic to manage servers.
 
AWS, by contrast, handles this automatically.
 
**My choice:** For cost-sensitive users, Contabo makes sense. For high-uptime requirements, AWS.
 
### Real-Time Execution
 
The connection between accounts for copy-trading and handling concurrency is critical. I manage this using **TCP connections** to emit and listen for events between accounts. This ensures execution is as fast as possible.
