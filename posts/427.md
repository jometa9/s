I built a small app called Bisbi. It's free, open source, and it lives here:

- Landing: [jometa9.github.io/bisbi-landing](https://jometa9.github.io/bisbi-landing/)
- Code: [github.com/jometa9/Bisbi](https://github.com/jometa9/Bisbi)

What it does is simple. You hold a key, you talk, you let go, and the text you just said shows up wherever your cursor is. Any app — Slack, the browser, my editor, a terminal. Any language. Nothing leaves your machine. That's it.

I've used it every single day for months now, and mostly for one thing: writing prompts.

Here's what changed. When I typed my prompts, I kept them short. Not because the short version was better — because typing is slow and I couldn't be bothered. So I'd write the minimum, hit enter, get a mediocre answer, and try again. Over and over.

It's easy to blame the model when that happens. But the model was fine. The prompt was the problem — short and lazy, because typing made it lazy.

Now I just talk. And when you talk, you don't care that the prompt is long. You say everything — the full context, the detail, the thing you actually mean, not the shrunk-down version you could stand to type. It takes seconds. Long prompts, better answers.

That's the whole trick. The speed is nice. But the real win is that I finally write the prompt I mean.

So here it is. Free, no accounts, no subscriptions, Mac and Windows. If you write to an AI all day like I do, try it.

Hold the key. Talk. Done.
