## The Ideas Behind It

Before the steps, the concepts behind them. These matter more than the rules — if I only copy the steps without understanding why they're there, I won't be able to tell a bad trade from bad luck later.

### How candles are built

When price is trending bullish, higher timeframe candles often **open bearish** first. That early move down is what becomes the bottom wick of a candle that closes bullish.

So the wick is not noise. The wick is where the buyers got filled.

That bottom wick is the best place to enter a long. Same thing inverted for shorts: the top wick of a bearish candle is where the sellers got in.

On the 1 minute chart, that wick is a whole move — a push down, a sweep, a reversal. That's what the model is trading.

### Over-extension

Price has to expand **away** from my bias for **at least 20 minutes**, without meaningful pullbacks along the way.

| ✅ Good | ❌ Bad |
|---|---|
| One clean push in one direction | Choppy back and forth |
| No real pullbacks inside the move | Price already pulled back twice |
| 20 minutes or more | A few candles |

The reason: a move that goes one way without pulling back leaves an imbalance behind it. That imbalance creates the *need* for price to come back and rebalance to around the 50% level. That return is the trade.

If price already pulled back on the way, the need is gone. No trade.

This is probably the condition I'll be loosest with at the start — the one where 12 minutes starts looking like "close enough". Worth noting in the journal when I do it, and seeing later whether it actually mattered.

### Market Structure Break (MSB)

An MSB is a **close past the wick high or low of the recent price leg**. It signals a possible reversal.

But for this model an MSB alone is not enough. Price **must take out local structure first**, and only then break the other way. Lower low, then break the high. That sequence is what makes it a valid **"type 3 shift"**.

Sweep first, break second. If the break comes without the sweep, it's not the setup.

### DXY correlation

Gold and the dollar normally move opposite each other. So:

- Gold up + DXY down → **bullish bias**, confluence.
- Gold down + DXY up → **bearish bias**, confluence.
- Both moving the same direction → **no confluence**.

When there's no correlation, the setup is lower probability. His rule is to skip it.

## The Steps

### Step 1 — Bias

Look at the 1 hour chart on gold and on DXY. Decide bullish or bearish.

If both are moving in tandem, the bias is weak. Skip the low probability conditions.

### Step 2 — Wait for one of two things

Along with **20+ minutes of expansion** away from my bias, one of these:

1. The **first hour of Asia low being swept**, when the bias is bullish (or the high swept, when bearish).
2. Price **rebalancing a recent trending move**. Extra confluence if there's a 30m candle sweep, 30 minutes or more into the second hour of Asia.

### Step 3 — Wait for the type 3 shift

On the 1 minute: local structure taken out, then the MSB.

No shift, no trade. This is where the patience is.

### Step 4 — Entry and stop

- **Entry:** around the **50% pullback of the MSB move**.
- **Stop loss:** at the **high or low of the type 3 shift**.

### Step 5 — Take profit

One of these, decided before I'm in:

- The **50% equilibrium of the over-extension move**.
- Or a **fixed 1.5R**.
- Or a broader target, but only when the bias is strong.

Three options means this is where most of my own decision-making lives. So it's the part worth recording carefully — which target I took, and where the others would have landed. That comparison is something the journal can answer for me later.

## Other Rules

- For bias I can also look at recent 1 hour candle formations. An engulfing first hour of Asia makes a continuation in that direction more likely.
- Higher timeframe ranges that need to rebalance can be another confluence. Anything I actually understand is worth adding; anything I'd just be copying isn't, at least not yet.
- **Maximum 2 attempts per session.**
- **Done for the day after one win.**
- Around **30% of the trades will be missed**, because price never gives the pullback for the entry. That's part of the model, not a failure of mine.

## The Checklist

The quick reference, so I'm not re-reading this whole post mid-session. This is also the list I'll copy into the journal for every trade:

```
[ ] Bias identified on 1hr gold + DXY
[ ] 20m+ expansion away from my trade direction
[ ] 1hr candle H/L sweep OR range rebalance
[ ] Type 3 shift on the 1m (sweep, then MSB)
[ ] Entry at 50% of the MSB move
[ ] Stop at the high/low of the shift
[ ] Target: 50% of the over-extension, or 1.5R
```

The model says that when parameters are missing, the trade quality drops, and the aim is to take only the A+ setups. I'll take that as given for now — but marking which boxes were unchecked is exactly what turns it from advice into something I can verify with my own trades.

## What Happens From Here

Right now this is his model, copied. It becomes *mine* through the journal, not through me editing the rules before I've even traded them.

So the approach is: trade it close to as written, log everything, and let the data point at what needs to change. Not because changing things is forbidden — because right now I have no information to change them *with*.

Every trade gets logged against the checklist above: which conditions were there, which ones weren't, and what happened. Over time that should start answering questions like:

- Which condition tends to be missing on my losers.
- Whether the 1.5R target or the 50% equilibrium target works better for me.
- Whether the setups without DXY confluence are really worse, or just feel worse.
- How many setups I actually miss, compared to the 30% he says to expect.

None of those have an answer today. They only get one after enough trades, and that's fine — collecting them *is* the work right now.

That's what's different from the other times I did this. Before, I'd take a strategy, lose a few trades, and go rewrite it. This time the strategy stays put and the journal does the arguing.

Every trade goes on the demo account, through this checklist. See you in the updates.
