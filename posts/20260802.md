If you know me, at this time of my life I'm training around 5 to 7 times per week.

Not necessarily for health, but for my identity as a consistent person...

> "I train every day" = "I'm a consistent and disciplined person"

Counting my training at the gym, I have more than 8 years, and doing sports and activity in general, easily more than 15 years (I'm 28 years old).

At different times in my life I've played tennis and rugby, ridden skate and longboard, run, and lifted weights at the gym. At the end of the day, my favorite is training muscle at the gym. A place where you don't depend on the weather, just a shed with metal structures and weight plates.

Keeping up training 6 or 7 times a week is easy at this moment of my life, because I have my own schedule, with no specific hours for my job. But I'm looking for a new job, a 9 to 5. With those hours, I can't go train at 15:00 when the gym is empty, only at 7 am.

For this reason I created a simple but effective routine to maintain (and eventually grow) my physique, independent of the type of job and the time available in my week during specific periods of my life. I'm looking at this type of simple training as a lifetime training plan, at least the structure.

## The Basics

- Three times per week
- Focus on intensity, hard sets
- Consistency over time
- High protein diet

## The Plan

### Day 1

- Chest × 2 exercises
- Triceps × 2 exercises
- Lateral Delts × 1 exercise

### Day 2

- Back × 2 exercises
- Biceps × 2 exercises
- Rear Delts × 1 exercise

### Day 3

- Quads × 2 exercises
- Hamstrings × 2 exercises
- Front Delts × 1 exercise
- Calves × 1 exercise
- Abs × 1–2 exercises

Let's try it for 6 months (yes, six months, not one).
