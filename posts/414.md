*Written: July 18, 2026, 20:38* 

For years, changing strategy over strategy.

Chasing the result. Chasing the freedom. But never putting the work on the right things.

Never taking true responsibility for the process that makes a profitable trader.

Overcomplicating the process, and starting again. And again.

Shiny object syndrome, in trading.

But now, no more of this bullsh*t.

This is the reason for this post: the old Joaquin who chased profit doesn't exist anymore.

But first I have a process. Steps to do, in order.

I created a step-by-step plan to stop chasing and start focusing on the process. To be the trader who has a real edge on the markets.

But trading is not my main income today. So I need to fix some aspects of my life first, before I come back to this process.

This is hard for me to say and to write, but I need it. I need the truth.

Here are the steps. I post the updates every week.

1. Consolidate a job as a developer.
2. A stable routine with my job — around my first month working, my adaptation process.
3. Document, from now to the accomplishment of points one and two, every trade I take on the demo account following the strategy process (the trading strategy is written in another post). Focused on the execution, not the result.

When these points are completed, we analyze our trades, our results, and I make a decision about the next steps. Maybe continue improving and studying my edge based on the data collected, do more backtesting, or just start a small real account challenge to start with the next level.

This is the part that makes the difference.

**One strategy, one symbol, one session.**

**The process, not the result.**

**Learn from the data of my trades.**

**Journal every trade I take.**

See you in the updates.
